Japanese Cuisine

Anyone who has ever been to Japan knows it: you inevitably struggle for a formulation for the incredible variety, the sensational quality, originality and beauty of Japanese food. The wonderful thing is that Japanese food is not only consists of sushi and sashimi. Almost everyone knows this typical Japanese dish, but also vegetables and meat have, as well as rice and pasta a permanent place in the Japanese kitchen.

The traditional Japanese cuisine (Washoku) finds its bases especially in rice and miso Soup. The food of this category is not only connected to the tradition of Japan, but focused on seasonal ingredients. Traditional food often consists of Seafood and fish either grilled or also raw (sashimi or sushi) as well as pickled vegetables (tsukemono). In addition to rice, which is served in the morning, noon and evening, Noodles like soba and udon are among the classic ingredients of Washoku.

Vietnamese Cuisine

Vietnamese cuisine is an independent and versatile cooking tradition of the country of Vietnam, which has produced many typical dishes. It is historically strong influenced of Chinese cuisine. In the south of Vietnam, it is also influenced by the Thai, the Khmer as well as some of the Indian cuisine.

The staple foods in Vietnam are rice and rice noodles and a large number of different Kinds of vegetables. Fish and meat play a subordinate role in mass nutrition. A historical peculiarity, however, is the variety of possible meat dishes, which almost includes all edible animals and also some insect species. Unlike many others National cooking traditions are largely unknown in Vietnam. But they do exist among the Buddhists. Spices are used very versatile, but less spicy than in the adjacent Thai or Indian cuisine. It dominate more subtle combinations of Spices of many cultures, including spice cinnamon and anise in conjunction with the numerous meats.
